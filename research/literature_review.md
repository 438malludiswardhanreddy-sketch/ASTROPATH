## 1. Introduction
Autonomous road surface monitoring has emerged as a critical research focus area within the paradigm of Smart City development. Historically, municipal bodies in India have relied on manual patrolling and citizen complaints to identify road hazards such as potholes and structural cracks. This review analyses current research papers, technologies, and methodologies in UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) navigation, computer vision, and multi-sensor fusion systems.

---

## 2. Computer Vision on the Edge (UAV Object Detection)
Road damage identification is primarily processed as an object detection problem. Early researchers utilised classical image processing techniques such as edge-based segmentation (Canny edge detectors) and thresholding to detect potholes. However, these methods are highly susceptible to variations in lighting, shadows, and road surface colouration.

### 2.1. Deep Learning Approaches (YOLO Frameworks)
With the advent of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), real-time detection has transitioned to deep learning models. The **YOLO (You Only Look Once)** family of models, particularly YOLOv4-tiny and YOLOv8, represents the state of the art in balancing frame rate (FPS) and mean Average Precision (mAP) on embedded hardware.
*   *Strengths:* High processing speeds allow edge deployment on processors such as the Raspberry Pi 4 or NVIDIA Jetson Nano.
*   *Limitations:* Small objects at high altitudes suffer from low feature resolution, necessitating secondary verification layers.

### 2.2. Secondary Classification (MobileNetV2)
To minimise false positive rates generated by YOLO bounding boxes (often caused by tree shadows, oil stains, or dry leaf debris), modern architectures implement a dual-stage pipeline. MobileNetV2 is widely preferred for secondary binary classification due to its depthwise separable convolutions, which significantly reduce parameter count and memory footprint while maintaining high classification accuracy.

## 3. Multi-Sensor State Estimation & Sensor Fusion
UAV telemetry signals are notoriously noisy. When mapping physical defects to global coordinates, rely on raw GPS feeds yields coordinate offsets of up to 5-10 metres, rendering the mapped data useless for repair crews.

### 3.1. Kalman Filtering vs. Particle Filters
To resolve this, sensor fusion techniques are deployed:
1.  **Extended Kalman Filter (EKF):** Blends high-frequency inertial measurement unit (IMU) readings with low-frequency global positioning system (GPS) coordinates and barometric altimeter feeds. It assumes Gaussian noise distributions, linearising non-linear drone dynamics using Jacobian matrices.
2.  **Particle Filters:** Offer superior accuracy under non-Gaussian noise profiles but demand prohibitive computational resources, making them unsuitable for low-latency edge computers.

Our system implements a custom 3D Kalman Filter, ensuring reliable state estimation with minimal CPU overhead, preserving computing power for real-time inference tasks.

## 4. Dynamic Path Planning & Autonomic Collision Avoidance
UAV routing in congested urban environments requires reactive path planning algorithms. Standard offline algorithms like A* or Dijkstra cannot adapt to dynamic obstacles (such as birds) or sudden weather alterations (wind gusts and heavy monsoonal rainfall).

### 4.1. Real-Time Detour Algorithms
- **Vector Field Histogram (VFH):** Calculates polar obstacle density maps to find clear directions.
- **Dynamic Window Approach (DWA):** Optimises collision-free velocity profiles over short time horizons.

ASTROPATH builds on these concepts by classifying the detected threat (Tree, Wire, or Bird) and executing threat-specific spatial deviations:
*   *Trees (Static Obstacles):* Lateral deviations.
*   *Power Wires (Thin Obstacles):* Vertical climb profiles.
*   *Birds (Dynamic Obstacles):* Temporal wait states (hovering).

## 5. Integration of Citizen Sensing and Crowdsourcing
Recent literature highlights the importance of hybrid sensing systems. Relying solely on municipal drone flights is resource-constrained. Integrating crowdsourced citizen uploads (via mobile web apps) provides a secondary data stream. This dual-source architecture allows cross-validation: drone flights verify crowdsourced citizen reports, while citizen uploads highlight target sectors for subsequent drone surveys.

---

## 6. Synthesis and Gaps in Current Literature
While current research successfully addresses individual aspects of either pothole detection, Kalman filtering, or autonomous flight control, there is a distinct lack of unified edge-orchestrated frameworks. Most architectures offload raw video streams to high-power cloud servers, introducing severe latency and bandwidth bottlenecks. ASTROPATH addresses this gap by executing:
1.  Onboard real-time inference (YOLOv4-tiny + MobileNetV2).
2.  Edge sensor fusion (3D Kalman Filter).
3.  Autonomous safety-driven routing directly on the drone's computational module.

This ensures the drone remains resilient to network dropouts and municipal bandwidth constraints.
